"Dr. Muhammad Yunus, Nobel Peace Prize laureate and founder of Grameen Bank, is a global pioneer in poverty eradication and social innovation. Born in Chittagong, Bangladesh, he revolutionized banking by introducing microfinance offering small, collateral-free loans to empower impoverished individuals, especially women. His initiatives have lifted millions out of poverty and inspired similar models worldwide. Dr. Yunus also championed social business, focusing on solving societal problems through sustainable, non-profit-driven enterprises. His accolades, including the Nobel Peace Prize and the Presidential Medal of Freedom, reflect his enduring impact as a visionary leader driving change for a more equitable world."
